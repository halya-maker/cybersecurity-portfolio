# Foundations of Cybersecurity

## Certificate
Completed as part of the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate on Coursera.

This course introduced the fundamental concepts of cybersecurity and the role of security professionals in protecting systems, networks, and data.

---

## Key Topics Learned

### 1. CIA Triad
The three main principles used to protect information:

- **Confidentiality** – Ensuring sensitive information is only accessible to authorized users.
- **Integrity** – Ensuring data remains accurate and unchanged unless modified by authorized users.
- **Availability** – Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed.

---

### 2. Types of Cyber Threats
Common threats that organizations face:

- Malware
- Phishing attacks
- Ransomware
- Insider threats
- Social engineering attacks

---

### 3. Security Operations Center (SOC)
A **SOC (Security Operations Center)** is a team responsible for monitoring, detecting, and responding to cybersecurity incidents.

SOC analysts typically:
- Monitor security alerts
- Investigate suspicious activity
- Respond to incidents
- Protect organizational systems

---

### 4. Security Frameworks
Organizations follow structured frameworks to manage security.

Examples include:

- NIST Cybersecurity Framework
- ISO 27001
- CIS Critical Security Controls

These frameworks help organizations identify, protect, detect, respond, and recover from cyber threats.

---

## Tools Introduced

Examples of tools used by cybersecurity professionals:

- **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)**  
  Used to monitor and analyze security logs.

- **Log monitoring tools**  
  Help analysts detect suspicious activity in systems.

---

- Understanding cybersecurity fundamentals
- Identifying common cyber threats
- Understanding the role of a SOC analyst
- Basic knowledge of security frameworks
- Awareness of security tools and monitoring systems

This course provided the foundational knowledge required to begin a career in cybersecurity. It introduced the responsibilities of security professionals and the importance of protecting organizational systems and data from cyber threats.
